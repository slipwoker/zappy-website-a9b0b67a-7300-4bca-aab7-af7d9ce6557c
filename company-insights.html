Method questions, answered.

Transparency is at the core of our approach. Here's how we collect, verify, and present workplace insights to help you make informed decisions.

Where does the data come from?

Our data is sourced directly from current and former employees who voluntarily share their workplace experiences. We aggregate reviews, salary reports, and company ratings from verified contributors to build comprehensive company profiles.

How often is it updated?

Company data refreshes in real-time as new reviews and salary reports are submitted. Index scores recalculate weekly to reflect the latest trends, ensuring you always see the most current workplace insights available.

How do you handle biased reviews?

We use a multi-layer verification process including email confirmation, employment validation, and algorithmic pattern detection. Reviews flagged as potentially biased undergo manual review before publication, and we maintain strict guidelines against fake or malicious content.

Can I compare companies fairly?

Yes. Our salary comparison tool normalizes data by location, role, and experience level. Index scores use weighted algorithms that account for company size, industry benchmarks, and review volume to ensure apples-to-apples comparisons across different organizations.

What does an index score represent?

An index score is a composite metric combining culture ratings, compensation competitiveness, career growth opportunities, and work-life balance. Scores range from 0-100, with higher numbers indicating stronger overall employee satisfaction and workplace quality.

How do you protect privacy?

All reviews are anonymized before publication. We strip identifying metadata, aggregate salary data into ranges, and never share individual contributor information with employers. Your feedback remains confidential while contributing to the collective knowledge base.
